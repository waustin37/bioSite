About

Sean Carmon started dancing when he was 12 years old and has not stopped since. Starting in Beaumont, Texas, Sean has worked tirelessly to train in various styles of dance making him an incredibly versatile performer. He received the prestigious Gates Scholarship to attend any college in the country tuition free, and Sean chose to go to NYU. There he saw The Alvin Ailey American Dance Company for the first time and he know he had to be a part of that historic company. He immediately switched college, and opted to attend the training program at Ailey at Fordham University. There he trained with some of the greatest in the world, and proved to himself and his classmates that he was not just the best dancer in a small town, he was a world class dancer, period.
While in his final year at Fordham, Sean received a phone call from a casting director looking for an emergency replacement for one of the dancers in the Broadway revival of Les Cage Aux Folles. Sean rushed down to the audition and danced his heart out, booking him the job. He performed for the entire run of that show, while finishing school. When Les Cage closed he auditioned and was cast in The Phantom of the Opera. He only worked a month though, before The Ailey Company hired him as one of their company dancers. He danced with Ailey for seven years, touring the world. He then heard the siren call of Broadway once more and got booked for The Lion King. He has worked and worked and worked. His career is enviable to almost anyone in the arts.
